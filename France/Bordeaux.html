Welcome to Bordeaux

Bordeaux is the capital of Nouvelle-Aquitaine and world-renowned wine capital, surrounded by prestigious vineyards. This elegant port city along the Garonne River features stunning 18th-century architecture, the UNESCO-listed historic center, and innovative contemporary culture. From neoclassical Place de la Bourse with its reflecting pool to modern Cit√© du Vin museum, Bordeaux balances wine heritage with urban sophistication, gastronomy, and artistic vitality.

Places to Visit

Place de la Bourse

This magnificent 18th-century square represents Bordeaux's classical elegance with symmetrical buildings designed by royal architect Ange-Jacques Gabriel. The square overlooks the Garonne River and showcases neoclassical architecture at its finest. The harmonious proportions and riverside location make it Bordeaux's most photographed landmark. The square represents the city's 18th-century prosperity and maritime trading power when Bordeaux was France's premier port.

Miroir d'Eau

This innovative reflecting pool opposite Place de la Bourse covers 3,450 square meters with 2cm of water creating mirror effects. The world's largest reflecting pool alternates between mirror and mist states, creating magical atmosphere especially at sunset. Children play while photographers capture reflections of classical architecture. Completed in 2006, the Miroir d'Eau represents Bordeaux's contemporary creativity and successful urban renewal blending heritage with modern design.
