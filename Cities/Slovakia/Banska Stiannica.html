PLACES TO VISIT

Old Town

The historic center features winding streets, colorful houses, Renaissance and baroque buildings climbing the hillside. The UNESCO-listed town preserves mining town character with churches, burghers' houses, and castles. The compact center allows walking exploration revealing architectural treasures and atmospheric lanes. The Old Town represents Slovakia's best-preserved mining heritage and Renaissance urbanism creating a living museum of Central European history.

Calvary

This baroque pilgrimage site features 24 chapels ascending the hill depicting Christ's passion. Built in the 18th century, the calvary creates dramatic sacred landscape with chapels, churches, and stations of the cross. The pilgrimage route offers town views and spiritual journey. The calvary represents baroque religious art and Slovak Catholic traditions while providing unique architectural and spiritual experience combining devotion with landscape design.

Church of St. Catherine

This Gothic church dedicated to miners' patron saint represents the town's mining heritage. The church architecture and religious art reflect the wealth from precious metal extraction. St. Catherine served the mining community's spiritual needs and symbolized protection for dangerous underground work. The church exemplifies how mining prosperity translated into religious architecture and continuing traditions honoring the patron saint.

Trinity Square

The main square features the plague column, Old Town Hall, and surrounding historic buildings creating the town's social center. The square buzzes with cafes and events maintaining community traditions. Trinity Square represents Bansk√° ≈†tiavnica's civic life and serves as natural gathering point.
