Welcome to Bratislava

Bratislava is Slovakia's capital and largest city, uniquely positioned on the Danube River bordering Austria and Hungary. This compact historic city features a beautifully restored Old Town, medieval castle overlooking the river, and charming streets with quirky sculptures. Once part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, Bratislava preserves Central European architecture while embracing post-communist transformation. The city's small size allows walking between attractions while offering authentic Slovak culture, wine traditions, and vibrant cafe life.

PLACES TO VISIT

St. Elisabeth Church

This Art Nouveau church nicknamed the "Blue Church" features stunning blue-and-white exterior with majolica roof tiles and a distinctive cylindrical tower. Built in 1913, the fairy-tale appearance and harmonious proportions create one of Bratislava's most photographed buildings. The pastel blue facade and Hungarian Secession style represent early 20th-century artistic movements. The church exemplifies Art Nouveau ecclesiastical architecture and stands as a beloved Bratislava icon.

St. Martin's Cathedral

This Gothic cathedral served as coronation church for Hungarian kings and queens from 1563-1830. The distinctive green-tiled roof and golden crown atop the 85-meter tower symbolize royal heritage. The Gothic interior contains royal tombs and baroque side chapels. St. Martin's represents Bratislava's importance within the Hungarian Kingdom and preserves coronation traditions making it the city's most significant religious and historical monument.

Apollo Bridge

This modern asymmetric cable-stayed bridge completed in 2005 features a distinctive single pylon design. The elegant structure crossing the Danube represents Bratislava's contemporary development and architectural ambition. The UFO observation deck on the nearby SNP Bridge offers better views, but Apollo Bridge showcases modern engineering and Slovakia's EU-era infrastructure investment creating striking addition to the riverside skyline.
