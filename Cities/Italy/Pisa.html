Welcome to Pisa

Pisa is a Tuscan city famous worldwide for its iconic Leaning Tower, but offers much more beyond this architectural marvel. Once a powerful maritime republic rivaling Venice and Genoa, Pisa preserves its medieval glory in the Field of Miracles with four masterpieces of Romanesque architecture. The university founded in 1343 brought Galileo and other scholars, creating intellectual traditions continuing today. Beyond the tower, Pisa offers authentic Tuscan life, riverside walks along the Arno, and genuine Italian atmosphere.

Places to Visit

Leaning Tower of Pisa

This iconic 56-meter bell tower began leaning during construction in the 12th century due to soft ground. The tilt increased over centuries before stabilization work. Climbing the 294 steps offers unique tilted perspective and city views. The tower represents architectural miscalculation transformed into beloved landmark. Its fame overshadows the cathedral it serves, but the tower's distinctive lean and marble beauty make it one of the world's most recognizable monuments.

Piazza dei Miracoli

This UNESCO World Heritage square contains four architectural masterpieces: the Cathedral, Leaning Tower, Baptistery, and Camposanto cemetery. The harmonious Romanesque ensemble on green lawn creates stunning visual impact. The square represents Pisa's medieval power and religious devotion. The unified architectural style and spiritual significance make it one of Italy's most important medieval complexes, deservedly called the Field of Miracles for its artistic and architectural achievement.

Pisa Cathedral

This Romanesque cathedral features a distinctive facade with tiered arcades and bronze doors depicting biblical scenes. The interior contains Galileo's Lamp chandelier, beautiful mosaic apse, and Gothic pulpit by Giovanni Pisano. The cathedral represents Pisan Romanesque architecture at its finest and served as model for Tuscan church design. The harmonious proportions and artistic treasures showcase Pisa's medieval wealth and artistic patronage during its maritime republic glory.

Piazza dei Cavalieri

This elegant square features Palazzo della Carovana with its elaborate sgraffito facade by Vasari and the baroque Santo Stefano church. The square served as the medieval city's political center and later headquarters of the Knights of St. Stephen. Today it houses the prestigious Scuola Normale Superiore university. The Renaissance architecture and academic atmosphere represent Pisa's evolution from maritime power to intellectual center, showcasing the city's continuing scholarly traditions.
