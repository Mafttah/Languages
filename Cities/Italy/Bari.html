Welcome to Bari

Bari is the capital of Puglia region and a major Adriatic port connecting Italy with Greece and the Balkans. The city combines the atmospheric Bari Vecchia old town with modern shopping districts and seafront promenades. Famous for St. Nicholas Basilica housing the saint's relics, fresh orecchiette pasta made in the streets, and authentic southern Italian atmosphere, Bari offers genuine culture away from tourist crowds while serving as gateway to Puglia's trulli houses and baroque towns.

Places to Visit

Bari Vecchia (Old Town)

This medieval quarter features labyrinthine narrow streets where elderly women make fresh orecchiette pasta outside their homes. The atmospheric neighborhood includes churches, palaces, and authentic local life. Getting intentionally lost in these alleys reveals authentic Bari where laundry hangs overhead and children play soccer. The old town represents traditional Puglian life and maritime heritage in a genuine urban environment unchanged for centuries.

Basilica di San Nicola

This Romanesque basilica houses St. Nicholas relics (Santa Claus) brought from Turkey in 1087, making it major pilgrimage site. The church features Norman architecture with crypt containing the saint's tomb producing myrrh. The fortress-like exterior and harmonious interior showcase Puglian Romanesque style. The basilica represents Bari's religious importance and medieval prosperity through pilgrimage trade. Both Catholic and Orthodox Christians venerate the saint here creating unique ecumenical significance.

Via delle Orecchiette

This street in Bari Vecchia is famous for elderly women hand-making fresh orecchiette pasta outside their doors, a living culinary tradition. Visitors can watch the process, purchase fresh pasta, and experience authentic Bari culture. The street represents traditional craftsmanship and domestic economy unchanged for generations. The scene of women shaping pasta embodies Puglian food culture and community life in picturesque traditional setting.

Via Sparano

Bari's main shopping street features pedestrian pavement lined with boutiques, chain stores, cafes, and historic buildings. The elegant boulevard connects the old town with the modern Murat district and represents Bari's commercial vitality. The street buzzes with locals during the evening passeggiata. Via Sparano exemplifies modern Bari where shopping and socializing combine in a sophisticated urban setting balancing tradition with contemporary retail culture.

Corso Vittorio Emanuele

This elegant street separates Bari Vecchia from the modern city and follows the former city walls. The corso features beautiful buildings, shops, and cafes with views toward the sea. The street represents Bari's 19th-century expansion and serves as the boundary between medieval and modern urban planning. Walking the corso reveals Bari's architectural evolution from old town to rational grid while providing access to both districts.

Bari Cathedral

This Romanesque cathedral dedicated to San Sabino features a simple facade contrasting with the more famous San Nicola basilica. Built in the 12th-13th centuries, the cathedral showcases Puglian Romanesque architecture with beautiful rose window, crypt with relics, and medieval atmosphere. The cathedral represents Bari's ecclesiastical heritage beyond the Nicholas pilgrimage, offering quieter, contemplative space for appreciating Romanesque artistry and religious continuity.

Piazza Mercantile

This historic square in Bari Vecchia served as the city's medieval commercial center. The Column of Justice where debtors were publicly shamed and the Sedile palace where merchants met represent medieval trade traditions. Surrounded by cafes and restaurants, the piazza maintains social function. The square exemplifies how Bari preserves medieval structures while adapting them to contemporary use, creating atmospheric gathering space with historical depth.

Piazza del Ferrarese

This waterfront square between Bari Vecchia and the modern city features outdoor cafes and seafront views. The piazza buzzes with aperitivo culture and evening socializing. The location connecting old and new while facing the Adriatic creates perfect spot for experiencing Bari's Mediterranean atmosphere. The square represents modern Bari's embrace of outdoor living and social traditions while maintaining connection to the sea and maritime heritage.
