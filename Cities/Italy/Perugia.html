Welcome to Perugia

Perugia is the capital of Umbria region, a beautifully preserved medieval hilltop city offering stunning countryside views. This Etruscan-founded city features medieval walls, Renaissance palaces, and vibrant cultural life through its ancient university and annual jazz festival. Famous for chocolate production by Perugina company and the July Umbria Jazz festival, Perugia combines medieval atmosphere with student energy and artistic traditions. The layered history, authentic Umbrian character, and dramatic hillside setting make it central Italy's hidden treasure.

Places to Visit

Cattedrale di San Lorenzo

Perugia's Gothic cathedral features an unfinished facade and houses the Virgin Mary's wedding ring in the Cappella del Santo Anello. The cathedral's austere exterior contrasts with the decorated interior containing frescoes and the bishop's throne. The building represents Perugia's religious importance and medieval architecture. The location on Piazza IV Novembre makes it central to the historic center while the wedding ring relic attracts pilgrims and adds sacred significance.

Chiesa di San Michele Arcangelo

This unique circular church from the 5th-6th century represents one of Umbria's oldest Christian buildings. The Paleochristian round structure with its ancient columns creates unusual sacred space. The church showcases early Christian architecture adapted from Roman models. Though modest compared to later churches, San Michele's antiquity and architectural uniqueness make it significant for understanding Christianity's early development in Umbria and circular church design traditions.

Piazza IV Novembre

Perugia's main square features the Fontana Maggiore, one of Italy's finest medieval fountains with sculptural panels by Nicola and Giovanni Pisano depicting months, liberal arts, and biblical scenes. The square surrounded by the cathedral and Palazzo dei Priori serves as Perugia's social and civic heart. The fountain represents 13th-century Perugia's prosperity and artistic patronage. The harmonious medieval ensemble makes it perfect for experiencing the city's historic atmosphere.
