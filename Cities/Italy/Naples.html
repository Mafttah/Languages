Welcome to Naples

Naples is southern Italy's vibrant capital, a city of contrasts where ancient history meets chaotic modern life. Founded by Greeks, ruled by Romans, Normans, and Bourbons, Naples preserves layers of civilization in its historic center, Europe's largest. The birthplace of pizza, Naples offers unmatched culinary traditions, passionate street life, and proximity to Pompeii, Herculaneum, and the Amalfi Coast. Mount Vesuvius backdrop, baroque churches, and authentic Neapolitan character create an intense, unforgettable urban experience.

Places to Visit

Piazza del Plebiscito

Naples' grand ceremonial square features the Royal Palace, San Francesco di Paola church resembling the Pantheon, and vast open space perfect for events. Created in the 19th century, the hemispherical piazza represents Bourbon Naples' imperial ambitions. The colonnade and neoclassical architecture create impressive ensemble. The square serves as Naples' symbolic center where major celebrations and gatherings occur, showcasing the city's grand architectural aspirations and public life.

Spaccanapoli

This narrow straight street literally "splits Naples" following the ancient Greco-Roman decumanus. Walking Spaccanapoli reveals authentic Neapolitan life with laundry hanging overhead, street vendors, churches, and constant activity. The street passes through the historic center's heart showing baroque churches, palaces, and daily chaos that defines Naples. It represents the city's living history where ancient urban planning meets contemporary southern Italian street life.

Duomo di Napoli

Naples Cathedral houses the blood of San Gennaro, the city's patron saint. Three times yearly, the dried blood miraculously liquefies during ceremonies attracting huge crowds. The Gothic cathedral with baroque chapel of San Gennaro features frescoes, mosaics, and archaeological remains beneath. The cathedral represents Neapolitan religious fervor and the saint's protective role. The miracle tradition maintains medieval faith practices in dramatic public ritual.

Via Toledo

Naples' main shopping street runs from Piazza del Plebiscito through the Spanish Quarter to Piazza Dante. The pedestrian thoroughfare features shops, cafes, and constant crowds. The street climbs gradually revealing Vesuvius views. Via Toledo represents Neapolitan commercial energy and provides access to the Spanish Quarter's narrow alleys. The mix of elegant architecture and chaotic street life creates quintessentially Neapolitan atmosphere.

Via dei Tribunali

This ancient street following the Greek-Roman grid hosts pizzerias, churches, and intense Neapolitan life. The street features Naples' best pizza at legendary establishments like Di Matteo and Sorbillo. Baroque churches, underground Naples access, and constant activity make it essential for experiencing authentic Naples. The narrow street packed with scooters, pedestrians, and street food represents Naples' vibrant chaos and culinary excellence in historic setting.

Piazza Bellini

This bohemian square features Greek walls from ancient Neapolis, trendy bars, and outdoor cafes. The piazza attracts students, artists, and intellectuals creating lively nightlife and aperitivo scene. Ancient ruins visible at street level remind visitors of Naples' 2,800-year history. The square represents contemporary Neapolitan culture while archaeological remains connect to the city's Greek foundations creating unique blend of ancient and modern urban life.

Pompeii

This ancient Roman city frozen by Vesuvius' 79 CE eruption offers unparalleled glimpse into Roman life. Preserved houses, shops, baths, theaters, and even bodies showcase daily existence. Frescoes, mosaics, and artifacts reveal Roman art and culture. The archaeological site represents history's most famous disaster while providing extraordinary documentation of Roman civilization. The proximity to Naples makes it an essential day trip showcasing Roman life's immediacy and fragility.
