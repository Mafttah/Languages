Welcome to Malaga

Malaga is the vibrant capital of Costa del Sol, birthplace of Pablo Picasso, and one of the Mediterranean's oldest cities. This sunny port city combines ancient Phoenician roots with modern art museums, Renaissance cathedral with Moorish fortress, and sandy beaches with sophisticated culture. Famous for sweet wine, excellent museums including the Picasso Museum, and nearby hilltop villages, Malaga offers authentic Andalusian charm with year-round sunshine and Mediterranean lifestyle.

Malaga Cathedral

Known as "La Manquita" (the one-armed lady) because its second tower was never completed, this Renaissance cathedral was built between 1528-1782 on the site of a former mosque. The interior features stunning Baroque choir stalls, magnificent organ, and soaring columns. The rooftop tour offers spectacular city views. The cathedral represents Malaga's Christian reconquest while its incomplete state adds character and nickname beloved by locals.
