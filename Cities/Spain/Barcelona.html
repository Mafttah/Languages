Barcelona Cathedral

The Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia, Barcelona's Gothic cathedral, was built between the 13th and 15th centuries. Its elaborate Gothic facade, completed in the 19th century, features intricate carvings and gargoyles. The cathedral houses the crypt of Saint Eulalia, Barcelona's patron saint, and its peaceful cloister contains thirteen white geese representing the age at which the saint was martyred. The rooftop offers stunning city views.

This stunning example of Catalan Gothic architecture was built between 1329-1383 by the merchants and shipbuilders of the Ribera district. Unlike the ornate Barcelona Cathedral, Santa Maria del Mar features pure, harmonious Gothic lines with soaring columns creating an impression of light and space. The church's austere beauty and perfect proportions make it one of Barcelona's most beautiful religious buildings, beloved by locals.

Barcelona's famous beach neighborhood was originally built in the 18th century to house displaced fishermen. Today, Barceloneta combines traditional seafood restaurants and narrow streets with modern beachfront development. The sandy beach stretches along the Mediterranean, lined with bars and restaurants serving fresh paella. It's the perfect place to experience Barcelona's beach culture and enjoy sunset views over the sea.

This hillside park built on a former quarry features a stunning artificial lake with a massive concrete sculpture by Eduardo Chillida suspended above the water.
