Switzerland of Latvia

Welcome to Sigulda

Sigulda is Latvia's adventure capital in Gauja National Park, known as the "Switzerland of Latvia" for its scenic hills, forests, and medieval castles. This small town offers bobsled track, cable car over the valley, and three castles within walking distance. The Gauja River valley creates dramatic landscapes perfect for hiking, cycling, and winter sports. Sigulda combines natural beauty with medieval heritage and outdoor activities making it Latvia's top nature and adventure destination.

Places to Visit
Turaida Church
This wooden Lutheran church from 1750 serves the Turaida museum reserve. The simple red structure represents traditional Latvian church architecture. The church's location near Turaida Castle and association with the Rose of Turaida legend adds romantic element. The church exemplifies rural Latvian religious architecture while serving as part of the larger castle complex showcasing centuries of Latvian history and folk traditions.
Gauja National Park
Latvia's largest national park surrounds Sigulda with pristine forests, sandstone cliffs, caves, and the Gauja River valley. The park offers hiking, canoeing, cycling, and winter sports. Medieval castles, archaeological sites, and diverse wildlife enhance the natural beauty. Gauja represents Latvia's commitment to nature preservation while providing outdoor recreation. The dramatic landscapes and ecological diversity make it the country's premier nature destination.
