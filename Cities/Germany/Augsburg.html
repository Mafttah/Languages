About Augsburg

Augsburg is one of Germany's oldest cities, founded by the Romans in 15 BCE as Augusta Vindelicorum. Located in Bavaria along the Romantic Road, Augsburg flourished during the Renaissance as a major banking and trading center, home to the wealthy Fugger family. The city boasts magnificent Renaissance architecture, the world's oldest social housing complex still in use, and a rich cultural heritage. With its Roman origins, medieval splendor, and Renaissance grandeur, Augsburg offers a fascinating journey through two millennia of European history.

This impressive cathedral, with origins dating back to the 11th century, is one of the most significant churches in Bavaria. The cathedral combines Romanesque and Gothic architectural elements, featuring distinctive twin towers and a magnificent bronze door depicting biblical scenes. Inside, visitors can admire the world's oldest stained glass windows from the 12th century, depicting prophets in stunning detail. The cathedral also houses important artworks by Hans Holbein the Elder and medieval frescoes. Its crypt contains Roman foundations, connecting the cathedral to Augsburg's ancient past.

Rathausplatz (Town Hall Square)

Augsburg's magnificent central square is dominated by the Renaissance Town Hall, built by Elias Holl between 1615-1620 and considered one of the most important secular Renaissance buildings north of the Alps. The square features the Perlach Tower, offering panoramic views from its 70-meter height. The Town Hall's Golden Hall is a stunning ceremonial space with gilded ceiling, magnificent portal, and Renaissance splendor. The square also features the Augustus Fountain, the first of three Renaissance fountains along Augsburg's main street. Rathausplatz represents the pinnacle of the city's Renaissance prosperity and remains the heart of civic life.
