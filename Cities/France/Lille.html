Welcome to Lille

Lille is the capital of northern France's Hauts-de-France region, showcasing Flemish heritage and industrial transformation. This vibrant university city features beautiful Flemish baroque architecture, the historic Vieux Lille district, and renowned art museums. Close to the Belgian border, Lille combines French elegance with Flemish charm, excellent beer culture, and friendly atmosphere. The city's strategic location makes it a European crossroads accessible by high-speed rail from Paris, London, and Brussels.

Places to Visit

Notre-Dame de la Treille

This Gothic Revival basilica took over a century to complete, with its controversial modern facade added in 1999 featuring translucent marble allowing light to filter through. The interior combines neo-Gothic architecture with contemporary art. The church represents Lille's Catholic heritage and architectural evolution. The name honors the Virgin of Treille, Lille's protector. The basilica anchors Vieux Lille and showcases the bold blend of traditional and modern design.

Lille's main square honors native son Charles de Gaulle with his statue and features the ornate Vieille Bourse, Chamber of Commerce, and colorful Flemish buildings. The vast square hosts markets, festivals, and concerts. Outdoor cafes line the arcades creating lively atmosphere. The Grand Place represents Lille's commercial and social heart, combining Flemish architectural heritage with contemporary urban vitality in a magnificent setting perfect for people-watching and exploring the city.

Bois de Boulogne

Lille's largest park covers 50 hectares with a zoo, playground, rose garden, and citadel ramparts. The park offers tree-lined paths, open meadows, and the star-shaped Vauban fortifications. Perfect for jogging, picnics, and family outings, the park provides green escape from urban density. The peaceful atmosphere and variety of attractions make it Lille's most beloved recreational space, combining military heritage with natural beauty and leisure facilities.
