Welcome to Strasbourg

Strasbourg is the capital of Alsace region and seat of the European Parliament, embodying Franco-German cultural fusion. The city features stunning Gothic cathedral, picturesque La Petite France district with half-timbered houses, and centuries-old wine tradition. Strasbourg's strategic location on the Rhine made it contested throughout history, resulting in unique architectural blend and bilingual culture. The UNESCO-listed Grande √éle, Christmas markets, and European institutions make Strasbourg a fascinating crossroads of cultures and governance.

Parc de l'Orangerie

Strasbourg's oldest park features formal French gardens, a small zoo, stork reserve, and romantic lake. Created in 1804, the park offers shaded paths, playgrounds, and the Josephine Pavilion. The stork colony symbolizes Alsace, and free-roaming peacocks add charm. Located near European institutions, the park provides green escape perfect for walks, picnics, and rowing on the lake surrounded by elegant landscaping.

Place Gutenberg

This picturesque square honors Johannes Gutenberg who lived in Strasbourg and perfected movable type printing here. The statue of Gutenberg stands at the center surrounded by beautiful half-timbered houses including the 16th-century Kammerzell House. The square hosts part of the Christmas market and offers excellent photo opportunities. It represents Strasbourg's Renaissance heritage and role in printing revolution that changed human communication.

Parc de la Citadelle

This park occupies the site of Vauban's 17th-century star fortress, preserving the moats and earthworks. The park offers extensive green spaces, walking paths, and historic fortifications showcasing military architecture. The tranquil setting with water features attracts joggers, picnickers, and history enthusiasts. The park represents Strasbourg's strategic military importance while providing contemporary recreational space respecting historical heritage.
