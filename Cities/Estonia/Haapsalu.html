City of Shawls & White Lady

Welcome to Haapsalu

Haapsalu is a picturesque seaside town on Estonia's west coast famous for its medieval castle, therapeutic mud, and traditional lace shawls. This former aristocratic spa resort features wooden architecture, the White Lady legend, and relaxed coastal atmosphere. The castle ruins, charming old town with colorful houses, and seaside promenade create romantic setting. Haapsalu represents authentic Estonian small-town character combining medieval heritage with spa traditions and artistic crafts.

PLACES TO VISIT

Haapsalu Castle

This 13th-century Episcopal castle features imposing walls, cathedral ruins, and the famous White Lady ghost legend. The castle museum displays Haapsalu's history while the cathedral hosts concerts with exceptional acoustics. The ruins create atmospheric setting especially during August full moon when the White Lady allegedly appears. The castle represents medieval church power and Haapsalu's historical importance as episcopal seat.

St. John's Lutheran

This Lutheran church serves Haapsalu's Protestant community with traditional architecture and services. The church represents Estonian Lutheran traditions and religious continuity. The building showcases how smaller Estonian towns maintain their churches as community centers and spiritual anchors. St. John's exemplifies local Lutheran heritage while serving contemporary worship needs in authentic small-town setting.

Haapsalu Old Town

The charming old town features wooden houses, narrow streets, and preserved 19th-century spa resort character. The compact center allows easy walking exploration revealing colorful architecture and local shops. The Old Town represents Haapsalu's evolution from medieval episcopal seat to aristocratic resort. The atmospheric streets and authentic character make it perfect for experiencing small-town Estonian coastal life.
