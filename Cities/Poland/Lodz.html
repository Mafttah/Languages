Welcome to Lodz

Lodz is Poland's third-largest city, transformed from 19th-century textile powerhouse to contemporary cultural center. The industrial heritage visible in converted factories, mansions of textile barons, and multicultural history create unique character. Famous for the National Film School that trained renowned directors including Polanski, Lodz combines industrial architecture with vibrant arts scene. The revitalized Piotrkowska Street, one of Europe's longest commercial streets, exemplifies the city's successful transformation.

Places to Visit

Piotrkowska Street

Europe's one of the longest commercial streets stretches over 4 kilometers through Lodz's center. This pedestrian boulevard features eclectic architecture from Art Nouveau to modernist, reflecting the city's industrial boom. Converted factories, trendy restaurants, galleries, and street art create vibrant atmosphere. The street represents Lodz's transformation from textile to cultural capital while preserving 19th-century industrial heritage. The mix of old and new makes it Poland's most interesting high street.
