Welcome to Poznan

Poznan is one of Poland's oldest cities and the birthplace of the Polish state where the first Polish rulers were crowned. This dynamic city combines medieval Old Market Square with modern business centers, hosting major international trade fairs. The famous mechanical goats butting heads daily at noon in the Town Hall, Renaissance and baroque architecture, and vibrant student life create unique atmosphere. Poznan represents Polish history's beginnings while embracing contemporary development as an economic and cultural powerhouse.

Places to Visit

Stary Rynek (Old Market Square)

Poznan's Renaissance Old Market Square features colorful merchants' houses and the magnificent Renaissance Town Hall. At noon daily, mechanical goats emerge from the clock tower and butt heads twelve times, a tradition dating to the 16th century. The square buzzes with cafes, restaurants, and the pillory where criminals were once punished. The harmonious architecture and lively atmosphere make it Poland's most beautiful market square after Krakow.

Fara Church (Parish Church)

This baroque Jesuit church is Poznan's largest and most ornate with elaborate decoration, frescoes, and gilded altars. The impressive facade and opulent interior represent Counter-Reformation baroque splendor. The church showcases Polish baroque architecture at its finest with dramatic decoration designed to inspire faith. The central location and artistic richness make it essential for understanding Poznan's religious and artistic heritage during the baroque period.
