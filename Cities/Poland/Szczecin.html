Welcome to Szczecin

Szczecin is a major port city in northwestern Poland on the Oder River, near the German border. Once part of Prussia and heavily bombed in WWII, Szczecin combines reconstructed historic buildings with modernist architecture. The Pomeranian Dukes' Castle, waterfront boulevards, and extensive green spaces characterize the city. As a significant Baltic port and cultural center, Szczecin offers maritime heritage, concerts at the Philharmonic Hall, and authentic Polish atmosphere with less tourist crowds than major cities.

Jasne B≈Çonia

This vast urban meadow covering 24 hectares serves as Szczecin's central park and event venue. The open greenspace hosts concerts, festivals, and public gatherings. The expansive lawns, walking paths, and recreational facilities make it popular for relaxation and sports. Jasne B≈Çonia represents Szczecin's commitment to public green spaces and serves as the city's outdoor living room where residents enjoy leisure activities, concerts, and community events in an accessible urban setting.

Stare Miasto (Old Town)

Szczecin's Old Town was heavily damaged in WWII and selectively reconstructed. The area features the Town Hall, historic streets, and churches showcasing the city's medieval origins. Unlike complete reconstructions elsewhere, Szczecin's approach mixed historic and modern architecture. The Old Town represents the city's complex history and pragmatic reconstruction philosophy. The blend of old and new creates unique character reflecting Szczecin's practical approach to heritage.

Kasprowicz Park

This large park features landscaped gardens, the Rose Garden with thousands of varieties, and recreational facilities. The park offers peaceful walking paths, lake, and seasonal flower displays. Named after Polish poet Jan Kasprowicz, it represents Szczecin's green infrastructure and provides quality outdoor space. The well-maintained gardens and variety of plants create beautiful settings for relaxation while serving diverse recreational needs from sports to contemplation.

Szczecin Cathedral

This Gothic basilica dedicated to St. James features a distinctive tower and spacious interior. Rebuilt after WWII destruction, the cathedral serves as Szczecin's main Catholic church. The Gothic architecture and religious art represent the city's spiritual heritage. The cathedral's central location and architectural significance make it an important landmark. The reconstruction effort demonstrates Szczecin's commitment to preserving religious buildings despite enormous war damage.
