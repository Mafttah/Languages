Welcome to Wroclaw

Wroclaw is Lower Silesia's capital, a city of islands connected by over 100 bridges across the Oder River. The beautifully reconstructed Market Square, Gothic churches on Cathedral Island, and over 600 bronze dwarf statues scattered throughout create distinctive character. Once German Breslau, the city became Polish after WWII with new population. The university traditions, vibrant cultural life, and the charming hunt for dwarf figurines make Wroclaw one of Poland's most delightful cities with unique multicultural heritage.

Places to Visit

Stare Miasto (Old Town)

Wroclaw's Old Town centers on the Market Square (Rynek) with colorful burgher houses and the Gothic Old Town Hall. The square's unique architectural ensemble combines Gothic, Renaissance, and baroque styles. Reconstructed after WWII destruction, it represents Wroclaw's resilience. The pedestrian streets radiating from the square, underground passages, and constant dwarf hunt create engaging urban experience mixing history, culture, and whimsy.

St. Elizabeth's Church

This Gothic church features a 91-meter tower offering panoramic Wroclaw views after climbing 300+ steps. The church's brick Gothic architecture and strategic location make it a landmark. The tower provides best perspective on the Market Square and city layout. St. Elizabeth represents Wroclaw's Gothic heritage and serves as excellent viewpoint. The climb rewards visitors with spectacular vistas understanding Wroclaw's island geography and architectural ensemble.

Plac Solny (Salt Square)

This charming square adjacent to the Market Square features the flower market operating daily year-round. The colorful stalls selling fresh flowers create vibrant atmosphere and photo opportunities. Historic buildings surround the square with cafes and restaurants. The Salt Square represents Wroclaw's market traditions and adds romantic charm. The contrast between permanent flower displays and seasonal variations makes it beloved by locals and visitors seeking authentic market culture.

Wroclaw Rynek

Wroclaw's Market Square (Rynek) is Poland's second-largest after Krakow with the impressive Old Town Hall, colorful townhouses, and outdoor dining. The square hosts events, street performers, and serves as social center. The architectural variety from Gothic to Art Nouveau creates visual richness. The Rynek represents Wroclaw's commercial heritage and civic pride while functioning as living plaza where residents and tourists mingle in vibrant urban theater.
