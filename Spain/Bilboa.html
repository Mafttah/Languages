Welcome to Bilbao

Bilbao is the vibrant capital of the Basque Country, transformed from industrial port to cultural destination by the iconic Guggenheim Museum. This dynamic city nestled along the Nervi√≥n River combines cutting-edge architecture with medieval old town charm. Famous for world-class pintxos (Basque tapas), contemporary art, and distinctive Basque language and culture, Bilbao represents successful urban regeneration while maintaining authentic character and culinary excellence.

Casco Viejo (Old Town)

Bilbao's medieval heart features seven streets (Siete Calles) lined with traditional shops, pintxos bars, and historic buildings. This atmospheric quarter preserves the city's original layout with narrow alleys opening into charming plazas. The old town buzzes with locals enjoying pintxos crawls, especially along Calle Jardines. Ancient churches, the Santiago Cathedral, and the Ribera Market create a perfect blend of history, culture, and gastronomy in the city's most authentic neighborhood.

2

Donejakue Katedrala (Santiago Cathedral)

This Gothic cathedral dating from the 14th century is Bilbao's most important religious building. Built on the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route, the cathedral features a beautiful Gothic cloister, Renaissance doorway, and impressive interior. The cathedral represents Bilbao's medieval importance and remains the spiritual center of the old town. Its location in the Casco Viejo makes it central to exploring the historic quarter's winding streets and pintxos bars.

The area around the bridge, known as La Ribera, houses the famous Ribera Market. The bridge symbolizes Bilbao's trading history and remains an iconic landmark connecting the city's historic and modern areas, perfect for riverside walks.

Plaza Unamuno Miguel

This elegant square in the old town honors philosopher and writer Miguel de Unamuno. Surrounded by traditional Basque architecture and pintxos bars, the plaza serves as a popular gathering spot. The square's outdoor terraces fill with locals enjoying drinks and conversation, especially on weekends. Its central location makes it a natural meeting point and gateway to exploring the Casco Viejo's narrow streets, shops, and authentic Bilbao atmosphere.

Plaza Berria (Plaza Nueva)

This neoclassical arcaded square from 1849 is the social heart of Bilbao's old town. The symmetrical plaza with its covered walkways houses numerous pintxos bars and restaurants under the arches. Every Sunday, a book and coin market fills the square. Plaza Nueva is where locals gather for Sunday aperitifs, making it the perfect place to experience Bilbao's social culture. The elegant architecture and lively atmosphere make it unforgettable.

Plaza Bizkaia

This modern plaza near the Town Hall features the distinctive red Arcos de Calatrava sculpture and marks the administrative center of Bilbao. The square combines contemporary design with surrounding historic buildings. It serves as a venue for events and markets, and connects to the riverfront promenade. The plaza represents Bilbao's commitment to public spaces that blend modern art with functionality, creating gathering points throughout the transformed city.

San Juan de Gaztelugatxe

This dramatic islet with its hermitage reached by 241 stone steps is one of the Basque Country's most spectacular sites, located 35km from Bilbao. The 10th-century chapel sits atop the rocky outcrop connected to mainland by a stone bridge. The location gained fame as Dragonstone in Game of Thrones. The stunning coastal scenery, crashing waves, and challenging climb make it a memorable day trip offering Basque coast's wild beauty.

Its location near the Ribera Market and river connects to Bilbao's trading history and relationship with the sea.

Catedral de Santiago

Bilbao's Gothic cathedral, built between the 14th and 16th centuries, serves as a waypoint on the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route. The cathedral features a beautiful cloister, Gothic architecture, and Renaissance elements. Recently restored, it showcases medieval craftsmanship and religious art. The cathedral's central location in the old town and its historical importance as a pilgrimage church make it essential to understanding Bilbao's medieval religious heritage.
