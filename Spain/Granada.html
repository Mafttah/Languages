Welcome to Granada

Granada is a city of legends at the foot of the Sierra Nevada mountains, home to the magnificent Alhambra palace. The last Moorish stronghold in Spain until 1492, Granada preserves its Islamic heritage in the whitewashed streets of the Albaic√≠n, Arabic teahouses, and stunning Nasrid architecture. This enchanting city blends cultures, offering free tapas with every drink, flamenco in Sacromonte caves, and breathtaking mountain views behind medieval monuments.

Granada Cathedral

Built on the site of Granada's main mosque after the Christian Reconquest, this Renaissance cathedral was constructed between 1523-1704. The cathedral features a massive facade, soaring columns, and an exceptionally bright interior due to its white stone and abundant windows. The adjacent Royal Chapel houses the tombs of Catholic Monarchs Ferdinand and Isabella who conquered Granada. The cathedral represents the triumphant Christian architecture that replaced Islamic Granada.

Plaza de Bib-Rambla

This central square near the cathedral has been Granada's commercial heart for centuries. Originally the site of bullfights and festivals, today it's a pedestrian plaza filled with flower stalls, cafes, and restaurants. The square connects major shopping streets and serves as a popular meeting point. The fountain at its center and surrounding historic buildings create a pleasant urban space perfect for people-watching while enjoying Granada's famous free tapas tradition.
