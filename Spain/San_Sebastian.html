Welcome to San Sebastian

San Sebastian (Donostia in Basque) is an elegant coastal city in Spain's Basque Country, renowned for its stunning La Concha Bay, Belle √âpoque architecture, and world-class gastronomy. This sophisticated seaside resort features golden beaches, Michelin-starred restaurants, and vibrant pintxos culture. The city perfectly balances natural beauty with culinary excellence, traditional Basque culture with cosmopolitan charm, making it one of Europe's most refined and gastronomically celebrated destinations.

üìç Places to Visit

1

Parque de Cristina Enea

This beautiful 19th-century park features exotic trees, landscaped gardens, peacocks, and the Cristina Enea Palace. The 9-hectare park along the Urumea River offers peaceful walks through varied vegetation including cedars, magnolias, and ginkgos. The park houses an environmental education center and hosts cultural events. Its romantic design and mature trees create a green oasis perfect for escaping urban bustle while remaining minutes from the city center.

2

Monte Urgull

This forested hill topped by the Castillo de la Mota and Sacred Heart statue dominates San Sebastian's old town. The mountain features walking paths through woods leading to castle ruins and panoramic viewpoints over the bay. Historical cannons and fortifications recall the city's defensive past. The climb rewards visitors with spectacular 360-degree views over La Concha Bay, the city, and coastline, making it the best vantage point to appreciate San Sebastian's beauty.

3

Iglesia de San Vicente Church

San Sebastian's oldest church, built in the 16th century in Gothic style with later Baroque additions, stands in the old town. The church survived the devastating 1813 fire that destroyed most of the old quarter. Its stern stone facade contrasts with the elaborate baroque altarpiece inside. The church represents continuity with San Sebastian's past and remains an important religious landmark. Its central location makes it a natural landmark when exploring Parte Vieja's narrow streets.

4

Buen Pastor Cathedral

This neo-Gothic cathedral, completed in 1897, is San Sebastian's largest religious building with its 75-meter spire dominating the Egia district. The cathedral's impressive facade, stained glass windows, and spacious interior create a dramatic sacred space. Built during the city's Belle √âpoque expansion, it represents San Sebastian's growth beyond the medieval quarter. The cathedral serves as the seat of the Diocese of San Sebastian and hosts major religious ceremonies.

5

Santa Mar√≠a del Coro Basilica

This Baroque basilica in the heart of Parte Vieja features an elaborate facade with the city's patron saint. Built in the 18th century on the site of an earlier Romanesque church, the basilica showcases ornate Baroque decoration with its carved portal considered one of the Basque Country's finest. The church hosts important religious festivals and represents the old town's spiritual center. Its location amidst pintxos bars creates interesting contrast between sacred and culinary traditions.

6

Parte Vieja (Old Town)

San Sebastian's old quarter, rebuilt after the 1813 fire, features a grid of narrow streets packed with pintxos bars, traditional shops, and lively atmosphere. This compact area between Monte Urgull and the Urumea River represents the city's social and gastronomic heart. Every evening, locals and visitors bar-hop enjoying elaborate pintxos and txakoli wine. The combination of Belle √âpoque buildings, Basque culture, and world-class gastronomy creates an unforgettable experience.
