Welcome to Zaragoza

Zaragoza is the capital of Aragon and Spain's fifth-largest city, strategically located on the Ebro River. This ancient city founded by Romans preserves over 2,000 years of history with Roman walls, Moorish palaces, and baroque cathedrals. The stunning Basilica of Our Lady of the Pillar dominates the skyline, while the Aljafer√≠a Palace showcases Islamic architecture. Zaragoza offers authentic Spanish culture away from tourist crowds, famous for its tapas culture and the annual Fiestas del Pilar celebration.

Basilica del Pilar

This massive baroque basilica is one of Spain's most important pilgrimage sites and Zaragoza's most iconic landmark. According to tradition, the Virgin Mary appeared here to St. James in 40 AD. The basilica features eleven colorful domes, four towers reaching 91 meters, and frescoes by Goya. The Holy Chapel houses the venerated wooden statue of the Virgin. Pilgrims and visitors come year-round, especially for the October Fiestas del Pilar celebrating the city's patron saint.
