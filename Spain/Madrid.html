Welcome to Madrid

Madrid is Spain's capital and largest city, located in the heart of the Iberian Peninsula. This vibrant metropolis combines regal splendor with modern energy, featuring world-class art museums, magnificent palaces, grand boulevards, and lively plazas. Known for its late-night dining culture, passionate football fans, and beautiful parks, Madrid offers an authentic Spanish experience. The city's central location, rich Habsburg and Bourbon heritage, and contemporary dynamism make it one of Europe's most captivating capitals.

Puerta del Sol

Madrid's most famous square and the symbolic center of Spain, marking Kilometer Zero from which all Spanish roads are measured. This bustling plaza features the iconic clock tower of the Casa de Correos, where Madrile√±os gather on New Year's Eve to eat twelve grapes at midnight. The square is home to the statue of the Bear and the Strawberry Tree, Madrid's official symbol.

Plaza Mayor

This magnificent rectangular square, completed in 1619 during the reign of Philip III, is surrounded by three-story residential buildings with 237 balconies overlooking the plaza. The square has hosted royal ceremonies, bullfights, markets, and even executions during the Spanish Inquisition. Today, its arcaded walkways house cafes, restaurants, and shops.

3

Parque de El Retiro

Madrid's most famous park covers 125 hectares and offers a green oasis in the city center. Originally a royal retreat for Philip IV in the 17th century, the park became public in 1868. El Retiro features the beautiful Palacio de Cristal, the Monument to Alfonso XII overlooking the Grand Pond where visitors can rent rowboats, and the Rosaleda rose garden with over 4,000 roses.

Plaza de Cibeles

One of Madrid's most iconic squares, dominated by the beautiful Cibeles Fountain featuring the Roman goddess Cybele on a chariot pulled by lions. The fountain has become a traditional gathering place for Real Madrid fans celebrating victories. The square is surrounded by magnificent buildings including the Cibeles Palace and the Bank of Spain.

6

Templo de Debod

This authentic ancient Egyptian temple, dating back to the 2nd century BCE, was gifted to Spain by Egypt in 1968 as thanks for helping save Abu Simbel temples from flooding. The temple was dismantled and rebuilt in Madrid's Parque del Oeste. The temple's location offers stunning sunset views over Madrid.

7

Catedral de la Almudena

Madrid's cathedral faces the Royal Palace and took over 100 years to complete, finally consecrated by Pope John Paul II in 1993. The cathedral combines neoclassical exterior with neo-Gothic interior and features modern religious art, colorful ceiling frescoes, and beautiful stained glass windows.

8

El Rastro

Madrid's most famous open-air flea market has operated every Sunday and public holiday morning since the 17th century. Stretching along Ribera de Curtidores and surrounding streets in the La Latina neighborhood, El Rastro offers everything from antiques and vintage clothing to books, records, and artwork.

9

Casa de Campo

This enormous park covering 1,722 hectares is five times larger than New York's Central Park and Madrid's largest green space. Originally a royal hunting estate, Casa de Campo became public in 1931. The park features the Madrid Zoo and Aquarium, an amusement park, and a lake for boating.

10

Plaza de Oriente

This elegant square between the Royal Palace and the Royal Theatre is one of Madrid's most refined spaces. Created in the early 19th century by Joseph Bonaparte, the plaza features formal gardens, the equestrian statue of Philip IV, and twenty statues of Spanish monarchs.

This large square features the famous monument to Miguel de Cervantes with bronze sculptures of Don Quixote and Sancho Panza, Spain's most beloved literary characters. Recently renovated, the plaza offers green spaces, fountains, and pedestrian areas perfect for relaxation.

Campo del Moro

These beautiful gardens extending from the Royal Palace down to the Manzanares River offer one of Madrid's most romantic settings. Named after the Muslim troops who camped here during the 12th-century siege, the gardens were redesigned in the 19th century in English landscape style.

14

Calle Preciados

This major pedestrian shopping street connects Puerta del Sol with Plaza de Callao and is one of Madrid's busiest commercial areas. Named after the Preciados family who owned properties here, the street is lined with international fashion brands and department stores.

15

Church of San Antonio de los Alemanes

This 17th-century Baroque church is one of Madrid's hidden gems, featuring an extraordinary interior completely covered in frescoes from floor to ceiling. Every surface is painted, creating an overwhelming visual experience that rivals the Sistine Chapel in its completeness.

16

Royal Basilica of St. Francis the Great

This impressive church features one of the largest domes in Christendom, with a diameter of 33 meters, surpassing even St. Paul's in London. Built in the neoclassical style in the late 18th century, the basilica is decorated with paintings by Spanish masters including Goya.
