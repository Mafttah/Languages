About Dortmund

Dortmund is Germany's eighth-largest city and the economic heart of the Ruhr region, with a fascinating transformation from industrial powerhouse to modern cultural center. Once famous for coal, steel, and brewing, Dortmund has reinvented itself while preserving its working-class heritage. The city is home to Borussia Dortmund, one of Europe's most passionate football clubs, and boasts extensive green spaces, medieval churches, and innovative museums. With its blend of industrial history, sporting culture, and urban renewal, Dortmund represents the dynamic spirit of modern Germany.

Westfalenpark

One of Germany's most beautiful urban parks, covering 70 hectares of landscaped gardens, lakes, and recreational facilities. Created for the Federal Garden Show in 1959 and expanded in 1969, the park features the iconic Florianturm tower, rose gardens with over 3,000 varieties, and the German Rosarium. The park offers playgrounds, a miniature railway, and hosts the Festival of Lights with spectacular illuminations. During summer, the open-air theater presents concerts and performances. Westfalenpark is a green oasis perfect for families, joggers, and anyone seeking nature within the city.

Florianturm (Florian Tower)

This distinctive 220-meter telecommunications tower is Dortmund's most recognizable landmark and Germany's seventh-tallest structure. Built in 1959, the tower features an observation deck at 138 meters offering breathtaking 360-degree views over Dortmund, the Ruhr region, and on clear days, the distant hills of Sauerland. The rotating restaurant completes a full revolution every hour, allowing diners to enjoy panoramic vistas while eating. At night, the tower is beautifully illuminated. The Florianturm symbolizes Dortmund's post-war renewal and remains an essential viewing point for understanding the city's geography.

Reinoldikirche (St. Reinold's Church)

Dortmund's oldest church and the city's symbol, dedicated to the city's patron saint Reinold. This Lutheran church dates back to the 13th century and features a distinctive 112-meter tower that dominates the city skyline. The church combines Romanesque and Gothic architectural elements, with its tower rebuilt after World War II. Inside, visitors find medieval sculptures, a magnificent altar, and the relics of St. Reinold. The tower can be climbed for excellent views over the historic city center. Reinoldikirche represents Dortmund's medieval heritage and remains the spiritual heart of the city.

Marienkirche (St. Mary's Church)

This Gothic hall church, built in the 14th century, is considered one of the masterpieces of Westphalian Gothic architecture. The church is famous for its Antwerp Altar, a magnificent carved altarpiece from 1420 featuring 633 gilded figures depicting biblical scenes. The interior showcases beautiful stained glass windows and medieval artwork. Despite severe damage in World War II, the church was carefully restored, preserving its artistic treasures. Located in the city center near the marketplace, Marienkirche stands as a testament to Dortmund's medieval prosperity and artistic heritage.
